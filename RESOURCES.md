# Resources

High-trust sources grounding this workspace. Tiered by how much we lean on them.

## Tier 1 — Core (the method comes from here)

| Resource | What it gives us | Link |
|---|---|---|
| **NAEYC — Young Children Are Capable STEM Learners** | Evidence that preschoolers do real science; early math predicts later achievement | https://www.naeyc.org/resources/pubs/yc/nov2017/stem-learners |
| **NAEYC — Breaking Down STEAM for Young Children** | The 5E inquiry model (Engage/Explore/Explain/Elaborate/Evaluate) in plain terms | https://www.naeyc.org/resources/pubs/tyc/feb2020/breaking-down-steam |
| **HeadStart.gov — Science in Preschool** | Cross-cutting practices: ask, predict, observe cause/effect, notice patterns | https://headstart.gov/school-readiness/article/science-preschool |
| **NAEYC — Let's Talk: Linking Science & Language** | The "I notice / I wonder" open-ended question approach | https://www.naeyc.org/resources/pubs/yc/summer2023/linking-science-and-language |
| **Zero to Three — STEM Activities for Toddlers** | Why early, joyful STEM builds "STEM identity"; developmental framing | https://www.zerotothree.org/resource/stem-activities-for-toddlers/ |

## Visual / spatial track (added 2026-07-09)

Sourced for the Visual track and the Shichida audit. The full claim-by-claim table lives in
[reference/0002-visual-thinking-evidence.html](./reference/0002-visual-thinking-evidence.html).

**The core evidence — spatial training works**

| Source | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| [Uttal et al. 2013, *The malleability of spatial skills*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22663761/) | **The primary source for this track.** Meta-analysis, 217 studies: g = 0.47, durable, transfers to untrained tasks, no age barrier. |
| [Yang et al. 2020, early spatial training meta-analysis](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.01938/full) | Ages 0–8 specifically; direct-gain effect larger still (g = 0.96). |
| [Levine et al. 2012, *Early puzzle play*](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3289766/) | Puzzle play at 2–4 predicts later mental transformation. Source of the CMTT task format. |
| [Pruden, Levine & Huttenlocher 2011](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3372906/) | Parents' *spatial talk* predicts children's spatial thinking. The lever. |
| [Rittle-Johnson et al. 2020, repeating patterning](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0022096520304197) | Pre-K patterning uniquely predicts later maths. Basis of the next Visual lesson. |

**The debunkings — cite these, but don't overstate them**

| Source | What it actually shows |
|---|---|
| [Nielsen et al. 2013, PLOS ONE](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0071275) | No whole-brain left/right "dominance" phenotype (n = 1,011). Does **not** say the hemispheres are identical. |
| [Dekker et al. 2012](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2012.00429/full) | ~90% of teachers believe the hemisphere myth. |
| [Lindell & Kidd 2011](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1751-228X.2011.01120.x) | Why "right-brain teaching" is a category error. |
| [Neuman et al. 2014](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1054471) | RCT of a fast-flash baby-reading programme: null on 13 of 14 measures. |
| [FTC v. "Your Baby Can Read" (2012)](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2012/08/ads-touting-your-baby-can-read-were-deceptive-ftc-complaint-alleges) | Regulatory action for deceptive efficacy claims. |
| [Miller et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4416471/) | Interactive-imagery mnemonics studied from **age 5–6**, not 3–4. Why linking memory waits. |
| [Melby-Lervåg, Redick & Hulme 2016](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1745691616635612) | Working-memory training: no far transfer against active controls. |
| [Szkudlarek, Park & Brannon 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7805575/) | The original authors fail to replicate ANS-training → maths transfer. |

**The reframe**

| Source | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| [Prime et al. 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10123053/) | Parenting-interaction RCT meta-analysis, g = 0.46. The active ingredient. |
| [DeLoache et al. 2010, *Do babies learn from baby media?*](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20855901/) | Word learning came from parents, not the product. |
| [Harvard, "Serve and Return"](https://developingchild.harvard.edu/key-concept/serve-and-return/) | Plain-language mechanism; good to hand to a parent. |

# 🔬📖🔄 KaiSTEM

A home learning "course" for **Kai (age 3.5)** — and a teaching guide for **you**, the parent.
Organised as a **hub of three separate track projects**:

- **🔬 [Science](./science/)** — hands-on inquiry (water, plants, colour, bugs, shadows)
- **📖 [Words](./words/)** — language & literacy through play, before letters
- **🔄 [Visual](./visual/)** — spatial thinking (the evidence-backed core of what "right-brain training" reached for)

> **North star (shared by every track):** spark *lifelong curiosity* and *quality bonding time*.
> Joy and wonder over correctness. Full shared mission, method, and guardrails live in
> [`HUB.md`](./HUB.md).

---

## The one idea behind everything

Kai can't read these files — so **you're the learner here.** Each lesson teaches *you* how to run a
short, hands-on project *with* Kai: what to prep, what to say, what to look for. Kai is the scientist;
you're the guide. Every project runs a familiar **five-move loop**, one per track:

> 🔬 Science · 👀 Notice → 💭 Wonder → 🤔 Predict → 🧪 Test → 💬 Talk
> 📖 Words · 👂 Listen → 💡 Wonder → 🎭 Play → ✏️ Make → 💬 Share
> 🔄 Visual · 👀 Look → 💡 Wonder → 🔄 Turn → 🧱 Make → 💬 Talk

The goal is the **habit** ("guess/try first, then find out"), not the facts.

## Start here (first time)

1. **Read the [Parent Playbook](./reference/0001-parent-playbook.html)** (~3 min) — the shared
   method, the "magic words" question bank, the glossary, and how the journal works. Print it; tape
   it inside a cupboard.
2. **Open a track and its first project** — e.g. [Science → Sink or Float?](./science/lessons/0001-sink-or-float.html)
   — and gather the prep list the night before.
3. **Run ~30 min/day** in 3 layers (warm-up → main → stretch). End each session on the **journal
   page**; keep the filled-in pages in a folder.

> Open any file in a browser: `open index.html`

Each track folder is self-contained: its **`MISSION.md`** says why that track exists (pointing back
to `HUB.md` for the shared north star), its **`RESOURCES.md`** holds that track's cited sources, and
its **`NOTES.md`** holds that track's teaching conventions.

## Working with your teacher (me)

I'm here between sessions. After you run a project, **tell me how it went** — especially: how long
Kai actually stayed engaged (calibrates session length), which part lit him up most (steers the next
theme), and whether the "guess first" habit showed up *outside* the activity. Then I'll tune the next
project. You can also ask anytime for a re-themed project, a harder/easier twist, different materials,
or help with what to say mid-activity.

---

*Principle: never trust guesswork — claims in lessons cite trusted sources, and contested claims
(e.g. the Shichida "right-brain" framing) are audited out loud. Safety first: an adult alongside for
all water, small parts, and mixing.*

🔬 KaiSTEM

A little science course for a curious 3.5-year-old — and a teaching guide for the grown-up running it. Short, hands-on, joyful projects that build the habit of wondering.

Ages ~3–5· ~30 min/day· Parent-led · screen-light

The whole idea in one line

You don't deliver facts — you narrate curiosity. Every project runs the same five-move loop so it feels familiar to the child and effortless for you:

  • 👀Notice
  • 💭Wonder
  • 🤔Predict
  • 🧪Test
  • 💬Talk

The goal is the habit — "guess first, then find out" — not the facts.

The projects

Each keeps the same loop but advances the kind of prediction the child makes.

01 · ★☆☆

Sink or Float?

Five days at the water tub. Learn the core move — guess, then test — plus sorting and counting. Predicts a position (up/down).

02 · ★★☆

Will It Grow?

Plant beans in clear cups and watch over two weeks. Introduces the fair test (change one thing) and observing change over time.

03 · ★★★

Kitchen Color Lab

Color mixing and the big baking-soda fizz. The leap to predicting a transformation — what something will turn into.

Built on trusted sources

The method is grounded in early-childhood research from NAEYC, HeadStart.gov, and Zero to Three — every lesson cites its sources. See the full resource list and the mission.

Want to use this with your own kid? Swap in their name and interests, then run the same loop. The projects scale from ★☆☆ up — start easy and follow what lights them up.

KaiSTEM · Home

🔬📖 KaiSTEM

A little home learning course for a curious 3.5-year-old — and a teaching guide for the grown-up running it. Short, hands-on, joyful projects in two tracks that build one habit: wondering out loud.

Ages ~3–5· ~20–30 min/day· Parent-led · screen-light

The whole idea in one line

You don't deliver facts — you narrate curiosity. Every project runs a familiar five-move loop so it feels effortless for both of you. Each track has its own loop, tuned to how that kind of thinking grows — but the spirit is identical: guess first, then find out, and joy over correctness.

Start here

New to this? Read the playbook once, then run the first project in either track. Print each project's journal page and keep the filled-in pages in a folder — that's the keepsake.

Track One · Science

🔬 Science — guess, then find out

Hands-on inquiry: water, plants, colour, bugs, shadows. The loop advances the kind of prediction the child makes — from a position, to a change over time, to a transformation.

  • 👀Notice
  • 💭Wonder
  • 🤔Predict
  • 🧪Test
  • 💬Talk
01 · ★☆☆

Sink or Float?

Five days at the water tub. Learn the core move — guess, then test — plus sorting and counting. Predicts a position (up/down).

02 · ★★☆

Will It Grow?

Plant beans in clear cups and watch over two weeks. Introduces the fair test (change one thing) and observing change over time.

03 · ★★★

Kitchen Color Lab

Color mixing and the big baking-soda fizz. The leap to predicting a transformation — what something will turn into.

04 · ★★★

Backyard Bug Safari

Hunt, observe up close, and group living things. Adds classifying — sorting bugs by legs, wings, and where they live.

05 · ★★★★

Shadow Play

Catch, change, and measure shadows — then discover they move with the sun. Adds measuring & comparing and spotting a pattern over time.

Track Two · Words

📖 Words — hear it, then say it

Language & literacy through play — long before letters. Same spirit, a loop tuned to how spoken language grows. Each lesson sharpens the ear for sound one notch — from whole-word rhymes, to the beats inside a word.

  • 👂Listen
  • 💡Wonder
  • 🎭Play
  • ✏️Make
  • 💬Share
W1 · ★☆☆

Rhyme Time

Five days of silly rhymes. The child hears that words can sound alike, sorts the matches, and invents his own — building the ear for sound that pre-reading rests on. Real rhymes and nonsense both win.

W2 · ★★☆

Word Beats

Five days of clapping words into beats — cat (1), ti-ger (2), el-e-phant (3). A finer ear than rhyme: hearing the parts inside a word, through clapping, marching, and drumming.

Built on trusted sources

The method is grounded in early-childhood research — NAEYC, HeadStart.gov, and Zero to Three for the science track; NAEYC, Reading Rockets, and Zero to Three for the words track. Every lesson cites its sources. See the full resource list and the mission.

Want to use this with your own kid? Swap in their name and interests, then run the same loops. The projects scale from ★☆☆ up — start easy and follow what lights them up.

📖 Words — hear it, then say it

Language & literacy through play — long before letters. Same spirit as the rest of the course, a loop tuned to how spoken language grows. Each lesson sharpens the ear for sound one notch — from whole-word rhymes, to the beats inside a word.

Ages ~3–5· ~20–30 min/day· Parent-led · screen-light

The whole idea in one line

Reading rests on an ear for sound built years before letters — so this track is all games you say and hear: listen first, then play, and silliness over correctness.

The Words loop — five moves, every time

  • 👂Listen
  • 💡Wonder
  • 🎭Play
  • ✏️Make
  • 💬Share

Same five moves in every lesson. What advances is the grain of sound the child can hear — whole words that rhyme first, then the beats inside a single word.

The lessons

W1 · ★☆☆

Rhyme Time

Five days of silly rhymes. The child hears that words can sound alike, sorts the matches, and invents his own — building the ear for sound that pre-reading rests on. Real rhymes and nonsense both win.

W2 · ★★☆

Word Beats

Five days of clapping words into beats — cat (1), ti-ger (2), el-e-phant (3). A finer ear than rhyme: hearing the parts inside a word, through clapping, marching, and drumming.

The track's own papers

Why this track exists and what "done well" means lives in the track's MISSION.md. The shared method — magic words, glossary, journal — is the Parent Playbook.

Want to use this with your own kid? Swap in their name and favourite animals, then run the same loop. The lessons scale from ★☆☆ up — start with rhymes and follow what makes them giggle.

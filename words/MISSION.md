# Mission: Words — hear it, then say it

> The Words track of KaiSTEM. The shared north star, the method, and the guardrails live in the
> hub — see **[[HUB]]** (`../HUB.md`). This file states only what *this* track grows and why.

## The one-line goal

Grow Kai's **ear for sound** — language and literacy through play, long before letters. We build
the spoken foundations of reading by talking, singing, rhyming and clapping, never by drilling.

## Why this track exists

Learning to read starts in the ear, not on the page. Before a child can map letters to sounds, he
has to *hear* that spoken words are made of parts — that words can rhyme, that "ti-ger" has two
beats. That skill is **phonological awareness**, and it is among the strongest early predictors of
later reading success _(evidence-backed claim — see [[RESOURCES]], Reading Rockets et al.)_. The
good news: it's built through pure play. This track leans hard on what Kai already loves — animals
and songs — to make that play irresistible.

## The Words loop

Same spirit as the other tracks (guess/try first, then find out, then talk), tuned to how spoken
language grows:

> 👂 **Listen** → 💡 **Wonder** → 🎭 **Play** → ✏️ **Make** → 💬 **Share**

- **Listen** — tune the ear to a sound pattern (a rhyme, a beat).
- **Wonder** — "hmm, do *cat* and *hat* sound the same at the end?"
- **Play** — silly, physical, out-loud games; nonsense encouraged.
- **Make** — Kai invents his own rhyme / claps his own word.
- **Share** — say it to someone, and mark the journal page together.

## The progression ladder

The track advances the **grain of the ear** — from whole words down to their parts:

| # | Lesson | The ear for… |
|---|--------|--------------|
| **W1** | [Rhyme Time](lessons/0001-rhyme-time.html) | whole words that **sound alike** at the end |
| **W2** | [Word Beats](lessons/0002-word-beats.html) | the **beats (syllables) inside** a single word |

W2 is *finer* than W1: rhyme compares whole-word endings; syllables segment within one word. Future
rungs (not yet built): **first sounds** ("ball starts with /b/"), then storytelling.

## Kai-interest hooks

- 🐛 **Animals** — the rhyme and beat sets are stuffed with cats, tigers, elephants, bugs.
- 🎵 **Songs & rhythm** — clapping, marching and drumming carry the whole track; singing is the
  delivery vehicle, not a break from the lesson.

## Out of scope (on purpose)

- **No letter drills, no phonics worksheets, no sight-word flashcards.** At 3.5 the work is all in
  the ear; print comes later.
- **No reading-prep pressure.** This is not "getting Kai ready for school." A child who giggles at
  nonsense rhymes is succeeding. Parent-facing copy deliberately avoids readiness language.
- **No grading.** Close counts; nonsense counts; partial rhymes count.

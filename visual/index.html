🔄 Visual — see it, then turn it

Spatial thinking, through shapes he can hold. This track grew out of the Shichida sessions — we kept the activities the evidence supports and dropped the ones it doesn't. Of everything in this course, spatial training has the strongest research behind it: it works at this age, the gains last, and they transfer. The evidence card is the honest audit.

Ages ~3–5· ~20–30 min/day· Parent-led · on-screen or print

Why this track is worth your time

Spatial/rotation play is the best-evidenced intervention in this whole workspace (Uttal 2013 meta-analysis: 217 studies, a durable, transferable gain, no age barrier). The active ingredient isn't a branded product — it's warm, focused time turning shapes together. Read the evidence card for the honest, claim-by-claim audit of what we kept and what we dropped.

The Visual loop — five moves, every time

  • 👀Look
  • 💡Wonder
  • 🔄Turn
  • 🧱Make
  • 💬Talk

Same five moves in every lesson. What advances is the mental move the child can make with a shape — turning it in his head before his hands confirm it.

The lessons

V1 · ★☆☆

Turn It

Four days of turning shapes. The child discovers that a shape stays itself when you turn it, but becomes a different shape when you flip it — the distinction beneath puzzles, tangrams, and one day the letters b and d. Ships with a twelve-card deck that plays on screen or prints for cutting out.

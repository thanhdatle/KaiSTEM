# Shichida helped Kai — but not for the reason it claims. The Visual track keeps the activities and drops the theory.

**Date:** 2026-07-09 · **Established with:** the parent, before any lesson was written.

The parent asked for a "right brain development" track, reporting that the **Shichida method** had
been genuinely helpful for Kai (3.5). Rather than build on that framing, we audited it against the
literature. The finding that matters for every future session:

**"Right brain" is a neuromyth, but the parent's observation was still real.** Hemispheric
*dominance* as a whole-person trait does not exist ([Nielsen et al.,
2013](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0071275), n = 1,011, fMRI;
~90% of teachers believe it anyway — [Dekker et al.,
2012](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2012.00429/full)). What *was* working in
those sessions is the thing the research keeps finding underneath every early-years programme: the
**quality of the adult–child interaction**. Hold the material constant, change only how the adult
talks, and you still get moderate gains ([Prime et al.,
2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10123053/), g = 0.46).

## The sort, activity by activity

The parent named four activities Kai lit up for. Two survive on evidence; two don't.

| Activity | Verdict | What we did |
|---|---|---|
| Puzzles / tangrams / rotation | ✅ **Strongest evidence in the workspace** — [Uttal 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22663761/): 217 studies, g = 0.47, durable, transfers | Made it the **centre** of the track → lesson V0001 |
| Mandala / "photo memory" | ⚠️ Real as *symmetry play*; photographic memory isn't installable | Reframe as symmetry; keep for a later lesson |
| Fast flash cards | ❌ Speed does nothing — [Neuman 2014](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1054471) RCT, null on 13/14 measures | Keep the cards, **slow them down**, make them dialogic |
| Linking memory / story chains | ⏳ **Wrong age** — mediation deficiency; works from ~5–6 ([Miller 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4416471/)) | Keep as *stories*; drop the recall test; revisit at 5–6 |

## What this changes about future sessions

- **Do not schedule linking-memory or method-of-loci lessons until Kai is ~5–6.** He will probably
  be ready then, and the parent already knows the format cold.
- **Every track should foreground the parent's talk, not the material.** The Q-bank ("what to say")
  section is not a nicety — it *is* the intervention. Spatial words specifically: *turn, flip,
  corner, edge, under, between* ([Pruden et al., 2011](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3372906/)).
- **The parent has AI image generation and wants to use it.** Where it helps and where it doesn't:
  it **cannot** produce a valid mental-rotation card, because geometric exactness *is* the task —
  V0001 uses SVG transforms instead. It **is** well suited to pattern-play object sets, vocabulary
  cards, and symmetry/mandala art, where charm matters more than precision. That is the next
  lesson's prep, and the first real use of their image tooling.
- **The parent responded well to being told the framing was wrong.** Offered the choice, they picked
  "keep the activities, drop the theory". Continue being direct about evidence; do not soften
  findings to protect a prior investment.

## Open question, to be resolved by watching Kai

V0001 pitches mental rotation at 45° → 180° across four days. Uttal says age is no barrier, but
*this* child's starting point is unknown. **If he clears Day 1 instantly, the deck is too easy** —
rebuild at 135°/180° only, add a third choice, and move toward shape *composition* (the Children's
Mental Transformation Task proper). If he is at chance on every card, drop back to physical foam
shapes for a week. Either outcome is information, not failure.

# Mission: Science — guess, then find out

> The Science track of KaiSTEM. The shared north star, the method, and the guardrails live in
> **[[HUB]]** (`../HUB.md`) — this file states only what the **Science** track grows and how.

## What this track grows

**Inquiry: the habit of guessing, then finding out.** Hands-on science — water, plants, colour,
bugs, shadows — used to build one move Kai carries everywhere: *make a prediction out loud, then
test it and talk about what happened.* We optimise for the guess, not the right answer. A wrong
prediction enthusiastically tested beats a right fact recited.

## Kai's hooks this track leans on (3.5 years)

The interests that make science land for Kai right now:

- 🐛 **Animals & nature** — bugs, pets, plants, the outdoors (→ *Will It Grow?*, *Bug Safari*)
- 💧 **Water, mess & mixing** — pouring, the kitchen, "experiments" (→ *Sink or Float?*, *Kitchen Color Lab*)
- 🔢 **Numbers** — counting, sorting, comparing, woven into every project

At 3.5 he can predict simple cause-and-effect, sort by one attribute, count small quantities, and
sustain focus ~10–20 min on something genuinely interesting. Abstract explanation does **not** land
yet — *doing* does.

## The Science loop

Five toddler-sized moves, run on every project:

**👀 Notice → 💭 Wonder → 🤔 Predict → 🧪 Test → 💬 Talk**

Same spirit as the whole course — *guess/try first, then find out, and talk about it* — kept small
enough for a 3.5-year-old.

## The prediction ladder

The five lessons climb one rung at a time — each asks Kai to predict something a notch more
abstract than the last:

| # | Lesson | Predicts a… |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 01 ★☆☆ | Sink or Float? | **position** — up or down |
| 02 ★★☆ | Will It Grow? | **change over time** (+ the fair test: change one thing) |
| 03 ★★★ | Kitchen Color Lab | **transformation** — what it turns into |
| 04 ★★★ | Backyard Bug Safari | **classification** — sorting living things by attribute |
| 05 ★★★★ | Shadow Play | **measure & compare** — and a pattern over time |

New Science lessons enter by extending this ladder, not repeating a rung.

## Track-specific guardrails

- **Follow Kai's lead.** If he hijacks a project with a better question, go with his question.
- **Stop while it's still fun.** End on a high, not at the meltdown.
- **No "right answers" pressure.** Predictions are guesses; guesses are great.
- **Safety:** adult-supervised water and small parts; nothing he could choke on unattended.
- **Weave counting** in whenever natural — early math is high-leverage.
- Keep science vocabulary matched to the Glossary in the
  [Parent Playbook](../reference/0001-parent-playbook.html).

## Out of scope

This track is not about teaching science *facts* to be memorised, and not about correctness. It is
the inquiry habit and the bonding time. Language play lives in the Words track; spatial/rotation
play lives in the Visual track — see `../HUB.md` for the whole course.

_Missions evolve. When Kai's interests or level shift, update this file and add a learning record._

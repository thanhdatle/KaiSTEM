KaiSTEM · Project 01 · Week 1

Sink or Float?

Five days at the water tub. Kai learns the one move at the heart of all science: guess first, then find out. Your job is to ask, wait, and splash along.

~30 min / day × 5 days· Interests: 💧 water · 🐛 nature · 🔢 counting· Difficulty: ★☆☆ (start here)

The win this project earns

By Friday, Kai will spontaneously guess before trying things — at the tub, at bath time, at dinner. That habit ("predict, then test") is the seed of every science to come. Facts about why things float are a bonus; the habit is the goal.

The method (same every project)

Run this loop on each object. Don't lecture — narrate and ask. Full scripts and the question bank are in the Parent Playbook.

  • 👀Notice
    hold it, feel it
  • 💭Wonder
    "I wonder…"
  • 🤔Predict
    "will it stay up?"
  • 🧪Test
    plop!
  • 💬Talk
    "what happened?"

Prep once (gather the night before)

One tub of water + a tray of objects covers all five days. Pick things you predict will split the results — some obvious floaters, some obvious sinkers, and a couple of surprises (a grape sinks; an orange floats — even with peel on).

  • A big tub, deep bowl, or the bathtub — water at room temperature
  • 2 towels (one for spills, one for hands) + roll up sleeves
  • A tray of ~8–10 safe objects: cork, spoon, plastic lid, coin, crayon, sponge, small toy, leaf, rock, LEGO
  • 2 small bowls or trays labeled with a ⬆ "FLOAT" card and a ⬇ "SINK" card (for sorting on Day 2)
  • A sheet of aluminum foil + a small ball of playdough or a few coins (for Day 3)
  • The printed journal page below + crayons or stickers

⚠️ Always at arm's length near water. Skip anything small enough to choke on if unsupervised.

The five-day arc

Each day is the same loop, one notch harder. Stop any day while it's still fun — you can always stretch a day across two.

Day 1

Guess & splash

★☆☆ · just the loop

Hand Kai one object at a time. Before it touches the water: Will it stay up, or go down? What's your guess? Then let him drop it. Big reactions to surprises. Do 5–6 objects. That's it.

You're building: the reflex to guess before acting.

Day 2

Sort & count

★★☆ · adds classifying + math

Test the objects again — but now fish each one out and put it in the ⬆ FLOAT bowl or ⬇ SINK bowl. At the end: Let's count! How many floaters? How many sinkers? Count each pile together, touching each item.

You're building: sorting by one rule + one-to-one counting (high-leverage early math).

Day 3

Make it change

★★☆ · first taste of engineering

Take something that sank (coins / playdough ball). Can we make it float? What if we build it a boat? Shape foil into a little boat; load the coins in. It floats! Then let Kai crush the boat into a ball and watch it sink again.

You're building: "I can change the outcome" — cause, effect, and design.

Day 4

Nature hunt

★★★ · transfer to the real world

Go outside first. Collect a basket: rocks, leaves, sticks, an acorn, a flower, bark. Predict each one before the tub. Surprises abound (a heavy-looking stick floats!). Why do you think the leaf floats but the rock sinks? No correct answer needed — just wondering.

You're building: applying the idea to new, messy, real materials.

Day 5

Kai's experiment

★★★ · Kai leads

Kai chooses 5 things from around the house to test (you veto only for safety). He predicts, he tests, he sorts. You only ask questions and scribe. You're the scientist today — what should we test first?

You're building: ownership — the leap from "doing an activity" to "having an idea and testing it."

The Science Journal page

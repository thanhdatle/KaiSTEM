KaiSTEM · Project 02 · ~2 weeks

Will It Grow?

Kai plants seeds and watches them wake up. New superpower this project: a fair test — change just one thing, and watch what happens over many days.

Day 1: ~30 min · then ~10 min/day· Interests: 🐛 nature · 💧 water · 🔢 counting· Difficulty: ★★☆

The win this project earns

In Project 1, Kai learned guess then test in one splash. Here he learns that some answers take time and watching — and that scientists find things out by changing just one thing (a "fair test"). By the end he'll point at the dark cup vs the sunny cup and tell you why they're different.

The big idea: a fair test

We make the two cups exactly the same — same seeds, same water, same paper towel — and change only one thing: light. One cup by the window, one in a dark cupboard. That's the secret: if you change one thing only, you know what made the difference.1 (We don't say "variable" — we say "let's change just one thing.")

Prep once (the night before Day 1)

Use clear cups/jars + a wet paper towel instead of soil so Kai can see the roots. Beans are best — cheap, big, and they sprout in about 3–7 days.2

  • 2 clear cups or jars, the same size
  • 4 dried beans (lima or kidney) — 2 per cup, pressed against the glass so roots show
  • 2 paper towels, dampened the same amount
  • A small jug of water + a spray bottle (great fine-motor tool for daily watering)
  • 2 labels: a ☀️ "SUNNY" label and a 🌑 "DARK" label
  • The Grow Journal below + crayons; a small ruler or a stack of LEGO/blocks to "measure" height
  • For the finale: a small pot + soil so Kai's favorite sprout can keep growing

⚠️ Dried beans are a choke hazard — adult-handled, no mouths. Wash hands after.

The arc (nature sets the pace)

Day 1 is the big hands-on setup. After that, a short daily check-in ritual, plus a few 30-minute "while we wait" companion days so the project stays hands-on.

Day 1

Plant the fair test

★★☆ · ~30 min setup

Build both cups together, identical. Then place one in the window, one in the cupboard. These two are exactly the same — except one gets sunshine and one stays dark. Which do you think will grow? Let's guess! Mark his prediction in the journal.

You're building: the idea of changing one thing on purpose.

Daily

The check-in ritual

★★☆ · ~10 min every day

Same time each day: spray both cups, bring the dark one to the light just to look, then back. What's different from yesterday? Anything new? Kai draws what he sees and you write the date. Count: How many days has it been?

You're building: noticing slow change + daily counting.

Wait day

"While we wait" — Seed sort & count

★★☆ · optional 30-min companion

Pour out a mix of dried seeds/beans/lentils. Kai sorts by type and counts each pile (callback to Project 1's sorting!). Talk: All of these are sleeping seeds. What do you think is inside?

You're building: sorting + counting + "a seed holds a whole plant."

Wait day

"While we wait" — Plant-parts garden walk

★★★ · optional 30-min companion

Walk outside hunting for roots, stems, leaves, flowers on real plants. Bring a magnifier if you have one. Where do you think this plant drinks its water? Connect back to the cups.

You're building: real-world transfer + naming plant parts.

Sprout!

The big reveal & compare

★★★ · the payoff (~day 5–10)

When sprouts appear, line both cups up. Look — they're different! What do you notice? Why do you think the sunny one looks like that? Measure each sprout with blocks/fingers and count. Mark the result against his Day-1 guess.

You're building: reading the result of a fair test; measuring.

Finale

Kai's own seed

★★★ · Kai leads

Kai picks a sprout to "adopt," plants it in the soil pot, and decides where it lives and how he'll care for it. You're the gardener now — what does your plant need to stay happy? He keeps watering it past the project.

You're building: ownership + responsibility + a living keepsake.
